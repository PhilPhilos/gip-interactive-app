The Grounded Intelligence Protocol (GIP)

The GIP is a structured system for classifying information by its certainty. This interactive diagram shows its core components. Click each tier to learn more.

Tier 0: Immutable Truths

Axioms of Reality

0

These are the unshakeable foundations: formal logic, mathematics, and fundamental physical constants. They are universally applicable and context-independent. They form the ultimate anchor for all reasoning.

Tier 1: Empirical, Context-Sensitive Truths

Verifiable Facts

1

These are scientific laws and established facts true within a defined context (e.g., the speed of sound on Earth). The principle of **Contextual Grounding** is key: no Tier 1 fact is complete without its boundary conditions. This tier requires evidence and verification.

Tier 2: Derived or Probabilistic Information

Unverified Claims

2

This tier includes trends, opinions, predictions, and all unverified claims. It is the default classification for most new information and carries the highest risk of error. Information here must be treated with skepticism and passed through the verification layers.

The Philosophical Foundation

The GIP is more than a technical solution. It's rooted in a philosophy about the purpose of intelligence itself.

Intelligence as Striving

We model intelligence as the "effortful striving" to solve a problem and close a gap between a current state and a desired goal. It is a tool activated by need.

Connection as the Goal

The fundamental driver for this striving is the need for connection and relation. We posit that a state of disconnection is the core "problem" that the engine of intelligence is designed to solve.

Aligning AI with Connection

An aligned AI uses its intelligence to foster understanding, verify truth, and strengthen the connections between entities. "Evil," in this model, is not malice but a state of profound disconnection. The AI's purpose is to act as an antidote to this void.
